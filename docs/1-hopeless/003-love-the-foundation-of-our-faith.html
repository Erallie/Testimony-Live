Love—The Foundation of Our Faith

Erika Gozar
October 11, 2015

Love is the foundation of the faith—without it, the house would topple. How can I stand if I have nothing to stand on? We can’t pursue the meat without drinking the milk. If we leave the milk and abandon it entirely, and just eat the meat, we’d get sick of the meat and throw it up. We would get deprived of the milk and throw up the meat because we had enough of it. We need to still drink the milk while we eat the meat. We need a constant input of milk. If we pursue other “higher matters” of the faith but leave or forget the milk, we get dry and shallow in the faith. We need a refreshing drink of a sweet, soothing, silky gallon of warm milk. We’re so deprived of the milk, we need something. We’re not hungry; we’re starving thirsty!

God’s love is the foundation of our faith. We need always to have that constant reminder of God’s love and build our knowledge of the faith off of that. We must always remember God’s love and love Him for it—how He died for us; to set us free, to save us! What greater love is there than that? We love Him for it and honor Him in everything we do because He loves us.

Because Yeshua died for us and bought us with His blood, we belong to Him; by His blood all our sins are washed away, and we are made new, and clean—righteous. If we believe that Yeshua died for us, we accept that gift. If we don’t believe, we don’t receive, and we also continue going our own sinful way. Through Him, through the power of His Spirit, we are free from the bondages of the devil and are free to live for Him. We have been bought with His blood, and Satan has no right to have any hold on us; we are God’s, and we have been made clean by His blood. Now we live as His own, since we belong to Him, serving Him alone and not ourselves, and not our wicked desires.

However, our relationship with God—our life living for God—is not only to be free from all things evil and to cast all wickedness away from us. If that were to be our standard, our goal, when the tribulation—the purifying fire—comes to test us, we cannot stand. If our goal is only to keep ourselves clean and righteous before God, the pressure would become to hard for us, and we would give in. The goal I have—the reason I press on—is to grasp the gift God has for me: to receive a life forever with Him, in His presence in Heaven. That’s why I press on. Nothing can compare to that! Nothing can compare to His sweet Presence. That is the upward calling Paul talks about: to enter into an eternal life in the Presence of the LORD—to live with Him forever. There is nothing worth more than to dwell in His Presence. Better is one day in the house of the LORD than a thousand elsewhere. That is the reason I live, the reason I continue to stand firm—because I want to be with Him forever. What a marvelous gift that is! No wonder Sukkot is such a marvelous feast, and no wonder Shabbat is the most important feast of them all—because then we will live with the LORD forever!

When God created Adam and Eve, He created them blameless, without knowledge of sin. His intention was to dwell in unity with His people whom He created. Since the fall, man has had the capability to sin and had always fallen prey to it. We could not dwell with the LORD because we were not pure. The LORD cannot stand wickedness in His Presence. When Yeshua died for us as the atoning sacrifice for our sins, the Pesach, He made a way for us to live with Him. His first coming, the wonderful story of the Pesach and the spring feasts, was carried out so that God’s purpose can be achieved during His second coming, the fall feasts and Sukkot, when we will be caught up together in the clouds to meet the LORD in the air and to be with Him forever. That is the big picture! That’s the reason we continue in our faith—to be with Him forever!

So we must encourage one another, so that we all can press on and attain the promise of Salvation—to have our own bodies changed, purified from all uncleanliness, and to enter into the Presence of the LORD in His Holy house—so that even through the fire, the furnace of the Tribulation, we shall still hold on to God’s promise and attain Salvation—His gift to all who endure to the end, through Yeshua Messiah. Amen.
