(A Dream Journal Entry)

Saturday, April 18, 2015; waking time 7:12 a.m.

I was playing the role of Audrey in A Little Shop of Horrors. I was backstage, in my red and white gingham dress. I wandered into a classroom and sat down at a desk, joining other students who were watching a bright-looking video or movie. When I decided to join them, a voice said, “Are you sure?”

I answered, “Yes,” thinking I had time.

I sat there for a while, watching the video. I felt like I was doing nothing but passing time.

After a while, I remembered that I was supposed to be playing Audrey and needed to be onstage at any moment, so I rushed to the theater and changed into my yellow sundress, thinking I was supposed to wear it for my next cue. However, by the time I finished getting the dress on and went backstage, I missed my part. I went back to the Theater office and changed into the other dress to prepare for my next cue, hoping I wouldn’t miss it.

On my way out of the Theater office, I walked by the assistant stage manager. She told me, “Never mind the cues you missed. Just move on to what’s next.”

I said, “Okay,” and tried to do that.


While I waited for my next cue, I remembered that Seymour was supposed to be onstage when I entered for my part, so for a while I was trying to find out whether Seymour was onstage. At one point, I was accidentally seen onstage, and someone said, “And now, our very own Audrey is going to sing!” Hearing that, I sat down in character on the rock, or the seat, as though I were about to sing my part. The orchestra started playing “Part of Your World”. I could have started singing, but because I thought they were supposed to play “Suddenly Seymour”, I said in my mind, “That’s the wrong song,” and didn’t sing. After a pause in the music when I remained silent, waiting for the orchestra to play the right song, someone in the audience yelled, “Hey, look! That’s Miss Stanley’s song!”

Then, the audience applauded as though I had just finished my song, and everyone crowded around the courtyard in a festive commotion. I didn’t sing my song. I thought they were having a post-premier celebration. I went to the courtyard and joined the party.

I wanted to talk with people, but no one I passed greeted me. I saw Miss Stanley and Chase Graham. Chase showed something on his phone to Miss Stanley, but I couldn’t see what it was; the screen looked all white. Then, he put his phone back, and both walked away. Because they left, I also walked away, trying to find Chase again and trying to interact with people, but no one was interacting with me, and I couldn’t find Chase.


I was starting to lose control over where I headed. I walked backstage. By then, the crowd had mostly cleared up and gone home. I was possibly the only one left.

A voice said, “Don’t forget what I told you to do.”

I thought that the voice might have told me to get my tote bag, so I tried to retrieve it.

I was about to pick it up, but the voice said again, “Don’t forget what I told you to do.”

“Did you tell me to get my tote bag? Is that what you told me to do?”

“You forgot.”

Then, a vision of the music video started playing in my mind. It was similar to how it played when I first received the idea. I was afraid that if I watched the whole thing right then, I might forget it, so before the introduction ended, (specifically after the first half of the introduction played) I said, “Wait!” Then the vision paused. A voice asked, “Why did you wait?”

“Shouldn’t I finish this part first, and then move on to the next?”

“Okay,” it said, and the vision went away. Then I woke up.
