All I Ever Do

4. Int. Living Area. Afternoon.

Fall 2016. ERIKA is standing in front of a wall between the kitchen and the living room in motionless indecision. She has been standing there for roughly a good twenty minutes. Her older sister NORA walks in from stage right and sees her standing there.

Nora

(indignantly)

Why are you just standing there Erika?! Don’t you have homework to do? (A SHORT BEAT) No—I know you have homework to do, so why aren’t you doing it?!

Their FATHER, who overheard the yelling from his office, walks in from stage left.

Nora (Cont’d)

And you’re just standing there catatonically as though you have nothing to do!

Father

(yelling)

She’s standing there because her mother’s filled her head with these crazy ideas! It’s craziness!

Nora

Craziness?

Father

(nodding incredulously)

Yeah! It’s crazy to just sit there and expect everything to just fall into your lap on a silver platter!

Erika backs up against the wall silently. Her eyes start tearing up.

Nora

(after a beat)

But… that doesn’t justify anything!

Father

Exactly! It’s ridiculous!

Nora

She shouldn’t just stand there!

Father

Uh-huh!

Nora

She should go and do something!

Father

Ridiculous!

Erika starts crying. Nora and Erika’s 10-year-old BROTHER wanders in from stage left and stands there silently listening into the conversation.

Nora

And she’s just standing there blankly staring at us, expecting all her homework to just magically get done?

Father

Uh-huh! Ridiculous!

Nora

That’s stupid! She can’t just expect her homework to magically complete itself! You can’t just stand there crying, expecting your homework to still get done by some miracle when you’re not even trying!

Brother

It’s useless just standing there Erika!

Nora

Why aren’t you trying?!

Erika

(sobbing)

I'm trying!

Nora, their brother, and the father all fall silent.

Erika (Cont’d)

(still sobbing)

Of course I’m trying! I’ve been trying day after day after day and nothing ever gets done, but I’m trying! I’m always trying! That’s all I ever do is try! (SNIFFLING) And you’re all standing there yelling at me saying I’m not trying, but I’m trying!

Erika brushes her back against the wall as she slowly lowers herself to the ground.

Erika (Cont’d)

(still sobbing)

I’m trying…

Father

(after a beat, as he turns to leave)

I’m sorry, how could I have known?

Nora

(after a beat, turning to leave)

Sorry. I didn’t know.

Brother

(turning to leave)

We didn’t know.

Nora, their brother, and the father all walk away, leaving Erika sitting there crying against the wall.
