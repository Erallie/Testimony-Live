Thursday, February 8th at 9:03 AM

Hello. I haven’t written a journal entry in a while, so I thought I’d do so again.

A lot happened since the last time I wrote. I started getting burnt out with the theater, so the past couple days, I’ve been working on learning the songs for the show instead of modeling and coding. The couple days prior to that, I was working on setting up the lighting board and making it work with the lights. I still haven’t set up the cue manager, so that you can record cues and save them to a text file that you can copy and paste elsewhere, but hopefully I will do that sometime later.

Because I was getting burnt out, I’ve decided that I would focus more on what I need for the show rather than the world itself. And then go back to the world if I’m feeling up to it again or if I get tired of the stuff for the show.

Also, if I haven’t noted this already, Jarod McGuire hit me up on the second this month. Asked if I wanted to hang out irl to catch up since he was back in Phoenix. We met up for lunch this past Monday. When I was there, I was anxious because I thought my brain was making me think I was liking him. And I didn’t want it to be that I stopped talking to him as a friend once another quick flame burns out. But it was partially because he contacted me on the second of February. And my brain was thinking, maybe this is what was meant when the voice said I’d “hear back in February”.

That whole thing was causing me anxiety during the second half of the hangout, when we went to the mall. And he could see it. And on the car ride home, he asked me what was up. I told him it was probably my medicine wearing off. But then after some silence, I started speaking, and then pretty immediately just said “Nevermind.” And then he said, “No, you have to say it now. You’ve opened Pandora’s box. Now you have to say it.” So I started saying a bunch of stuff that was all pretty incoherent and didn’t really interconnect, and I mentioned that it was probably incoherent at the end, and he said yes it was, but what’s important is that I was able to express myself. And then I started saying more stuff, and he eventually parked in the cul de sac so we could finish talking. And then somehow we got to a point where he responded and it would look sort of like some sort of conclusion to the whole thing, but I was still troubled, and he asked if he should pull into the driveway now, and I said “uh okay sure”, and he said “what do you want?”, “What is the problem?” And after a while of thinking I realized it had to do with Chase and hearing back in February. And I realized that this is something I don’t usually tell people unless I already trust them, but I decided to tell him anyway.

And so I told him the whole story, and also tied it back into how it related to that day. And he was able to chime in and lay out for me what exactly was going on and why my mind was doing certain things. And he just brought clarity to the situation. And it helped, although there was still a little bit of anxiety left. But afterward, it had probably already been an hour parked in that cul de sac, and he had things he needed to do to prepare for the next day, so he pulled into the driveway and let me go. He told me I could hit him up whenever I wanted to get out.

The following days, I think I was hyperfixated on him. Right after he left, and I changed and lay down on my bed to process things, I ended up sending him a wall of messages to summarize things. And then the following two days, I sent 6 different messages, each with different time stamps, and was anxious and worried that I was sending him too many messages. It wasn’t until I was praying last night—after a lot of crying—that God would take care of my concern about messaging him too much that God gave me the idea to message him asking if I was sending him too many messages. So I did that last night, and this morning he replied saying, “Yeah. A bit.” And so I said I’d back off a bit.

You can probably tell by the amount of text I dedicated to explaining this that this has been on my mind a lot the past couple days. Today, I think I’m able to not hyperfixate on Jarod. I just need other things on my mind to keep me busy.

This Saturday, I have a vocal lesson with Gail. I’ll probably work through some of the songs for the show. Otherwise, maybe I’ll work on old art songs and arias.

Again, I hope I don’t burn out about the rose theater.

Last night, I also was crying a lot. Around mid-afternoon, I just felt an urge to do something, almost similar to boredom. I was in a vc with Chaoticat. I went afk to change my shirt, and I started talking to myself, and I realized that it was less painful or agonizing to talk to myself, so after I came back to the call, I said goodbye again so that I could go and talk to myself.

I don’t really remember how the crying started, but it happened. I was almost worried that I’d lose my faith at one point. And I think God or another voice said the fact I was praying to Him right then meant that I wasn’t gonna lose my faith.

I recently prayed that God would fix my social life. Because I thought that it was possible for the rose theater to give me enough meaning in life, but that all that was missing was something to do in the evenings as well as having the emotional and social supports I needed, such as some type of bond with certain people having something to do with emotional trust, or something like that. I forgot how exactly I described it before. Maybe just something so that I’m not suffering alone, whenever suffering does happen. Perhaps. I don’t know. It’s confusing and pretty disheartening to think about.

Also, my period started yesterday. It’s possible part of the reason why I thought I liked Jarod was because of hormones.

I also remembered yesterday in the middle of the intense mental storm that I should be kind to my own thoughts in my brain. After I started doing that again, things became easier to bear again. I need to remember to keep doing that.

Anyway, I guess reflecting on this has reminded me of the sadness again, and now I’m feeling a little bit down again. Like, reflecting specifically on the crying. Perhaps I should take a break from the rose theater until I resolve whatever is getting me down emotionally. Because it’s definitely really hard to keep going with something when things are emotionally bringing you down. But then again, I don’t know how feasible it would be for me to resolve this on my own. But I guess there’s no other way to do it.

I shouldn’t say that. That might make me feel hopeless. I know I said that I’d have to reflect on my life again at the end of February, but that might end up happening sooner.
