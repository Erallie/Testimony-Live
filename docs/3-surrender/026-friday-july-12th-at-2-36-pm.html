Hello again. So it is officially over a month since I last called Garret.

I didn't write yesterday that I started writing a book. Some time after starting to write things on theprose.com, the challenge prompts that I felt like I could write about started running out, and it was taking a while for new prompts to show up. So I decided I wanted to write a book.

Let me backtrack. When I was writing stuff, I was sending them to a lot of people. One of them was Nil. He said something about one of my works that I was offended by, and I continued to behave saltily toward him with every further conversation until I forgave him. I think maybe a day or so before I decided to start writing that book, Nil was able to give me some understanding that helped me understand how to trust myself to be able to push through and keep trying when the weight of discouragement pulls me down. And it was that God gives me strength to continue trying and doing the things he wants me to do, through His Holy Spirit. After that conversation though, I wasn't quite sure I wanted to surrender and trust God with whatever he wants me to go through, because that would mean all the pain again. Since Nil has periodically been sending me gifs saying I'm doing a good job and stuff like that, I decided to tell him today that I'm writing a book. And just maybe within the past hour or so, I asked about how I can trust God throughout the difficult parts.

I think the difference is… trying makes things more painful. If you give up, you can avoid some of that pain. I know life is painful regardless, but sometimes it makes it less painful not to try. Or at least I think that's what it feels like. Because of the line from IRL, "Trying does nothing but cause pain [sic]." I remember that that resonated with me a lot last year months after having written the script.

Some of my reasoning was that pain is gonna happen regardless; it's just choosing whether to trust God to give me strength to push through or not. But now after typing this out, I realize that's not a good enough refutation.

Nil was saying stuff that reminded me of how I'm supposed to try to do things out of love. He said like, I have to think about what God would want me to do, and whether He'd want me to give up or keep trying. And that reminded me that I should try to love God. Or at least try to do things that are loving.

Anyway, back to maybe the day after Nil and I had that first conversation about trusting God to give me strength, that's when I decided to write a book. So I walked around and brainstormed a bit. And I talked with Nora to help me brainstorm, and Sara a little bit too, and it helped a lot. The book is called The Leaden Weight, and is framed as though it's written from a secondary character when the main character is the Princess Elira, but it's actually about her maidservant, Holkra. Both characters are based off of me, Elira when I was 18, and Holkra now. At the end of the story, Holkra learns that not even the leaden weight can weigh down the winds' breath, so she trusts the winds to carry her through and help her push past the leaden weight.
