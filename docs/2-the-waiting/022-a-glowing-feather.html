A Glowing Feather

Late June 2024

I tried. And I didn’t stop.
Although the walls were crumbling around me
And none of my efforts could save them from disintegrating to ash.

I tried. Despite the impossibility
Of my buckets of tears quenching the flames’ barrage;
I tried regardless,
As though there were still some hope to cling onto
Somewhere in the remaining cesspool of blackened snow.

I tried. Despite the ever-scorching heat
And the gallons of evaporating tears,
Despite the waning of my sight
And the steadily dwindling air,

I tried.
And I tried.
And I kept on trying.
Until I came to realize
That sometimes trying is futile,
And the effort isn’t worth the pain.
And then I fell to my knees amongst
The remnants of what once was home.

But when I woke within that pool of ashen tears,
However crippling the waste that was laid,
I found a glowing amber feather
That, upon my breath, began to rise
And float gingerly above the burnt sand.
It whispered a silent promise
Before dissolving serenely into golden mist,
As though imploring me to trust.

Now after many years, my home remains
No more than charred wood and scattered dust.
I don’t know if I want to try again.
But maybe, just maybe,
I’ll watch for that glowing feather,
And wait to rise once more.
